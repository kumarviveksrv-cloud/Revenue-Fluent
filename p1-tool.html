Revenue Connect
What it is · Why it matters · The 4 instruments
▼
What it is: Revenue Connect is the first of 5 Revenue Fluent pillars. It converts people movement — exits, vacancies, transitions — from a headcount percentage into a precise rupee consequence. ATC% measures the cost of exits relative to revenue. AR% measures the rate. TDF measures the drag. Together they tell you what attrition is actually doing to your business.

Why it matters: Every board responds to rupees. "14% attrition" triggers a nod. "₹1.1Cr in revenue at risk" triggers a budget decision. Revenue Connect gives HR professionals the number that changes the conversation permanently.

The 4 instruments: ATC% (Attrition Cost %), AR% (Attrition Rate), TDF (Talent Disruption Frequency), ESR (Execution Stability Ratio).
Attrition Cost % (ATC%)
—
Calculating...
Benchmark: <80% · Critical: >140%
Total replacement cost ÷ Annual revenue. Converts attrition from a headcount rate into a financial burden on revenue. A 141% ATC means you spend ₹1.41 replacing talent for every ₹1 of annual revenue.
Attrition Rate % (AR%)
—
Calculating...
Healthy: <10% · Warning: >20%
Exits ÷ Average headcount. Your headline attrition rate — but unlike a raw number, Revenue Connect connects this rate directly to its revenue consequence via ATC%.
Talent Disruption Freq. (TDF)
—
Calculating...
Healthy: <5% · High: >10%
Frequency of unexpected talent disruptions as % of headcount — unplanned exits, offer drops, early exits. Your early warning system for execution momentum fragility.

Target: >80% · Critical: <65%
% of workforce in a stable, execution-ready state. When too many people are ramping, in notice, or in transition — ESR drops and your real execution capacity falls below your headcount count.

📖 Understand this pillar — What it is · Why it matters · How to calculate it ▼
The Core Problem

Every HR professional knows attrition is expensive. But "expensive" without a number is just noise in a boardroom that runs on spreadsheets. ATC% converts attrition from a rate into a revenue burden. When TechVelocity's CHRO walked in with "14% attrition," the CFO nodded and moved on. When she ran Revenue Connect and returned with "₹1.1Cr in revenue at risk with 88% attribution confidence," she got a headcount budget increase.

The Formulas
ATC% = (Exits × Avg Salary × 2.0 multiplier) ÷ Annual Revenue × 100
AR% = Exits ÷ Average Headcount × 100
TDF = Disruption Events ÷ Headcount × 100
ESR = Stable Employees ÷ Total Employees × 100
The 2× Multiplier

Every exit costs approximately 2× the annual salary of the person who left. This breaks down as: 1× notice period at reduced productivity + 0.25× hiring cost + 0.75× new hire ramp at 50% productivity for 1.5 months. For senior or highly specialised roles, this multiplier rises to 3×.

What Good Looks Like

ATC% below 80% — healthy. Replacement cost is a manageable share of revenue. ATC% 80-140% — watch carefully. ATC% above 140% — critical. You are spending more replacing talent than the revenue benchmark can absorb. ESR below 65% signals execution fragility — too many people in transition to execute predictably.
