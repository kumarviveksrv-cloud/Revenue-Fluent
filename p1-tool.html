People-Revenue Connect
Every other function in the business can draw a straight line from its activity to revenue. Sales closes deals. Marketing generates pipeline. Product ships features customers pay for. HR has never been able to do this — it reports headcount, engagement scores, and attrition percentages that sit in a world disconnected from the P&L.
People-Revenue Connect is the first pillar of Revenue Fluency because it builds the bridge. It takes the people events HR already tracks — exits, tenure, replacement cost — and translates them into the language the board actually cares about: revenue protected, revenue at risk, and revenue lost.
When a senior account executive walks out the door, the cost isn't their salary. It's the pipeline that goes cold, the clients who hesitate, the ramp time before a replacement is productive, and the institutional knowledge that leaves with them. Revenue Connect quantifies all of it.
Why it matters
The moment HR can say "this attrition cost us ₹1.1Cr in revenue" instead of "our attrition rate is 14%," the conversation changes. One is an HR metric the CFO tolerates. The other is a business problem the CFO acts on.
Revenue Connect gives you three things no engagement survey ever could: a defensible number (revenue at risk), a confidence score (how strongly that number is attributable to people decisions), and a narrative the board understands without translation.
This is the foundation of every pillar that follows. People Alpha, Team Architecture, HUMANCE, and the People P&L all build on the core premise this pillar proves: people decisions are revenue decisions.
The 3 instruments
Revenue Connect runs on three measurement instruments. Each one isolates a different way people events translate into revenue impact.
Attrition Cost %ATC%
ATC% = (Total replacement cost of exits ÷ Annual revenue) × 100
Expresses the full replacement cost of departed talent as a percentage of revenue. It reframes attrition from an HR rate into a revenue drag. A high ATC% means you're spending a meaningful slice of revenue just standing still.
Benchmark: healthy <80% · watch 80–100% · critical >100%
Talent Drag FactorTDF
TDF = Revenue capacity lost to transitions ÷ Total revenue capacity
Measures how much revenue-generating capacity is dragged down by people in transition — ramping, backfilling, or covering gaps. The lower the drag, the more of your headcount is actually producing revenue right now.
Benchmark: healthy >0.5 · the higher, the less drag on capacity
Employee-Sales RatioESR
ESR = Revenue ÷ (Headcount × Avg fully-loaded cost) × 100
The efficiency instrument — how much revenue each rupee of people cost generates. When ESR drops after a people event, you can attribute the efficiency loss directly to that event with a quantified figure.
Benchmark: 80%+ threshold for a revenue-efficient org
