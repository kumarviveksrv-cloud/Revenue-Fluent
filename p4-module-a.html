The 4 HUMANCE Asset Classes

HUMANCE is not one number. It's a balance sheet with four distinct asset classes — each representing a different category of intangible human value. Click each to reveal the revenue proxy.

Asset Class 1
Trust Capital
Click to reveal value →
What HR builds: Psychological safety, manager credibility, team cohesion, institutional confidence.

Revenue proxy: Team velocity change + retention rate delta. A 10% increase in Trust Capital correlates with 8-12% improvement in team output velocity.

Mehta & Sons: Founder's Trust Capital represented 34 years of relationship equity. Its sudden absence triggered cascading exits worth ₹2.8Cr in HUMANCE destruction.
Asset Class 2
Culture Yield
Click to reveal value →
What HR builds: Culture sustained through disruption, reorgs, leadership transitions, and crises.

Revenue proxy: Productivity continuity during transitions. Culture Yield measures what % of normal output is preserved when the org is under stress.

Mehta & Sons: Culture Yield collapsed from 94% to 61% in 6 months post-succession. That 33-point gap = ₹1.1Cr in productivity HUMANCE destroyed.
Asset Class 3
Signal Intelligence
Click to reveal value →
What HR builds: Listening systems, early-warning pattern recognition, catching problems before they become crises.

Revenue proxy: Cost of the problem that didn't happen. The value of a crisis prevented is the full cost of that crisis — calculated retrospectively.

Mehta & Sons: HR flagged the succession risk 8 months before the transition. It was ignored. The cost of the ignored signal: ₹4.2Cr in total revenue impact.
Asset Class 4
Belonging Premium
Click to reveal value →
What HR builds: People feeling seen, valued, included — discretionary effort above contract.

Revenue proxy: Discretionary effort multiplier + employer brand value. People who feel they belong give 20-34% more than their contracted hours and output.

Mehta & Sons: Senior managers' Belonging Premium was tied to personal relationship with the founder. Post-succession Belonging Premium dropped to near zero — triggering resignation wave.
