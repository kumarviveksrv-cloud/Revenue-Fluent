The Revenue DNA Mapping Methodology

A 4-step process to identify your org's highest-performing team configuration and calculate the revenue cost of any structural deviation.

1
Map your team inventory
List every team in the org with: size, management ratio, decision layers, and quarterly revenue output. This is your baseline dataset.
Apex: 3 production teams, 18 operators, 1 supervisor. Revenue/team/quarter: €940K average before restructures.
2
Identify the outlier teams
Which teams consistently generate above-average revenue? What do they have in common structurally? Size, management ratio, decision speed, accountability clarity.
Apex DNA: Teams of 6 operators + 1 experienced supervisor → generated 112% of benchmark. This is the Revenue DNA signature.
3
Measure deviation cost
For every team that deviates from the Revenue DNA signature, calculate the revenue gap. Use OEI and PCoB to quantify the structural cost of the deviation.
Post-restructure: 3 supervisors for 11 operators (1:3.7 ratio). OEI indicates top-heavy → €1.1M revenue shortfall in H2.
4
Prescribe structural corrections
Present the Revenue DNA analysis to leadership. "This restructure moved us 26% away from our highest-performing configuration. To restore DNA: return to 1:6 supervisor ratio. Projected revenue recovery: €940K/quarter."
This is the HR conversation that changes strategy. Not engagement. Not culture. Revenue architecture.

The 4 Leadership Archetypes

Team configuration alone doesn't determine revenue. The leadership archetype running each configuration multiplies or destroys its potential. Match the wrong archetype to the wrong configuration and even Config A underperforms.

Archetype 1
The Builder
Creates clarity, removes blockers, trusts the team. Low ego, high output orientation. Gives autonomy before asking for it.
Best config: A (High-Autonomy Pod) → generates +34% revenue
Archetype 2
The Coordinator
Manages dependencies, aligns stakeholders, keeps large teams moving. Excellent in complexity, less effective in small autonomous settings.
Best config: B (Matrix Team) → generates +8% revenue
Archetype 3
The Optimiser
Data-driven, process-focused, incremental improvement orientation. Excels at squeezing efficiency from stable operations. Stifles innovation.
Best config: B or C in stable environments
Archetype 4
The Controller
Approval-seeking, risk-averse, bottleneck by design. Creates Config C dynamics regardless of team size. Revenue destroyer in high-velocity environments.
Toxic in Config A → destroys revenue advantage immediately
Apex Manufacturing – The Archetype Mismatch

Priya and Daniel were promoted from Operator (Builder archetype) to Supervisor. But the new supervisor role at Apex post-restructure was a Coordinator role – managing larger spans, aligning with the new director layer, attending planning meetings. Builder archetypes in Coordinator roles lose effectiveness and drain the autonomy from their teams. This is why output dropped even on the teams they supervised.
