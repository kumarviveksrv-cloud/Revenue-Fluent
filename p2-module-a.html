What is People Alpha?

In finance, alpha is the return your fund generates above the market benchmark. If the market returns 12% and your fund returns 16%, your alpha is +4%. That delta is everything in asset management.

The Finance Analogy
12%
Market return
→
+4%
Alpha generated

People Alpha applies the exact same concept to HR decisions. Every company makes roughly the same categories of people decisions — hiring, promotions, L&D investment, retention, team design. But some companies extract dramatically more revenue per decision than others.

That delta is People Alpha.

The Definition

People Alpha = The revenue your people decisions generate above (or below) the industry benchmark for equivalent decisions.

A +14% Hiring Alpha means your hiring decisions generate 14% more revenue-ready talent per quarter than the industry median. A -8% Promotion Alpha means you're promoting the wrong people and it's costing you revenue vs. what competitors extract from similar promotions.

The 5 Alpha Dimensions

People Alpha isn't one number. It's a scorecard across 5 dimensions — each measuring a different category of people decision against the market benchmark. Click each dimension to explore.

Dimension 1
Hiring Alpha
+14%
Do your hires generate more or less revenue than the industry median for equivalent roles?

A +14% Hiring Alpha means your selection criteria, interviewing methodology, and onboarding process combine to produce hires who reach full productivity 14% faster and generate 14% more revenue per quarter than industry equivalents. This is your hiring function's ROI expressed as a market-relative metric.

Sterling Consulting benchmark: Industry average revenue per new hire in Year 1 = £142,000. Sterling actual = £108,000. Hiring Alpha = -24% 🔴
Dimension 2
Promotion Alpha
-8%
Do your promotions increase or decrease team revenue vs. what the industry sees from similar promotions?

A -8% Promotion Alpha means your promotions are destroying value vs. the market. Industry data shows that well-calibrated promotions increase team revenue by an average of 12% in the following quarter. Your promotions are generating only 4% uplift — an 8 percentage point gap. You're promoting the wrong people, at the wrong time, into the wrong roles.

Sterling Consulting: 4 simultaneous promotions to Partner track in Q1 → billable utilisation dropped 18% → £2.4M revenue miss. Classic negative Promotion Alpha. 🔴
Dimension 3
Retention Alpha
+22%
When you retain someone, do they generate more revenue than a replacement would?

A +22% Retention Alpha means your culture is a quantifiable revenue asset. People who stay at your company generate 22% more revenue per year than industry equivalents at the same tenure — because your culture compounds their capability faster. This isn't a soft claim. It's a financial statement about your retention ROI.

High Retention Alpha companies compound talent like compound interest. Every year a person stays = 22% more than a replacement at Year 1. 🟢
Dimension 4
Team Design Alpha
-12%
Do your team structures generate more revenue per FTE than optimal configurations?

A -12% Team Design Alpha means your org design is leaving money on the table. The way you structure teams, set spans of control, allocate senior talent, and design handoffs generates 12% less revenue per FTE than the industry benchmark for comparable team compositions. This bridges Pillar 2 and Pillar 3 — Team Revenue Architecture.

Sterling: Too many partners relative to fee earners → high cost, low revenue per FTE → structural Team Design Alpha problem. 🔴
Dimension 5
L&D Alpha
+18%
Does your training spend generate more attributable revenue than the industry median?

A +18% L&D Alpha means your training function is a revenue engine, not a cost centre. For every £1 invested in L&D, your organisation generates £1.18 more revenue than the industry median for equivalent L&D spend. Your programmes are better targeted, better designed, and better transferred to the job than competitors.

L&D Alpha companies treat training budget as a futures position. Every pound invested today generates compound returns in capability. 🟢

Sterling's People Alpha Scorecard

Sterling Consulting Group promoted 4 consultants to Partner track simultaneously in Q1. Billable utilisation dropped 18%. Revenue miss: £2.4M. The board blamed "difficult market conditions." Let's read their People Alpha scorecard.

❌ What Sterling Did
→ Promoted 4 consultants simultaneously based on tenure, not readiness
→ No Partner readiness assessment. No calibration against peers.
→ Immediately pulled promoted consultants off billable work for "partner training"
→ No revenue target set for promoted partners in first 6 months
→ 3 of 4 underperformed. 1 exited within a year.
✅ What +Alpha Companies Do
→ Promote based on demonstrated performance at the NEXT level, not current level
→ Stagger promotions — max 1-2 per quarter to maintain team stability
→ Maintain 80% billable during transition with structured shadow-partnerwork
→ Set clear 90-day revenue targets with weekly check-ins
→ Promotion Alpha: +8% avg across industry peers who do this well

People Alpha as a Valuation Metric

Here's where People Alpha gets truly disruptive. When enough companies adopt it, investors start asking: "What's their People Alpha?" A company with +20% People Alpha is compounding human capital faster than competitors. That's not an HR metric anymore. That's a market metric.
