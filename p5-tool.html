The People P&L
This is the synthesis — the pillar that assembles everything. A profit & loss statement has one job: revenue in, cost out, net result. The People P&L gives HR its own income statement, built from the four pillars that came before it.
People Revenue (what your people generated and protected) minus People Cost (what they cost to employ and replace) equals Net People ROI — the single number that proves whether HR is a value creator or a cost centre, in the only language the board speaks fluently.
Marketing walks into the board with "9x ROI." Sales walks in with "10.5x." HR has always walked in with headcount and engagement scores. The People P&L is HR finally walking in with "2.3x Net People ROI" — and a statement to back it.
Why it matters
The People P&L is the deliverable that earns HR its strategic seat. It converts four pillars of intelligence into one CFO-grade statement that can be presented, defended, and tracked quarter over quarter like any other P&L.
It also makes the trade-offs visible. When attrition leakage or HUMANCE destruction drags the ratio below breakeven, the People P&L shows exactly where the value is leaking — and which pillar to fix.
This is the graduation pillar of Revenue Fluency. Once you can generate a People P&L, you can answer the ultimate question: for every rupee this company spends on people, how many rupees come back?
How it assembles
Each instrument isolates a different way this pillar translates people decisions into revenue impact.
People RevenuePR
PR = P1 Protected + P2 Alpha + P3 Architecture + P4 HUMANCE
The total revenue your people generated and protected, assembled from all four upstream pillars. This is the line HR has never been able to report — and the one that changes the board conversation.
Sourced from: Pillars 1 through 4 combined
People CostPC
PC = Total payroll (PCoB) + Attrition leakage
The full cost of the workforce — not just payroll, but the revenue that leaks out through attrition. Costing only payroll understates the true number and hides where value is lost.
Includes: payroll plus the hidden cost of attrition
Net People ROINPR
NPR = Total People Revenue − Total People Cost
The bottom line. A positive Net People ROI proves people are a net value creator; negative proves the opposite. This is the number that ends the "HR is just a cost" argument permanently.
Benchmark: positive = value creator · negative = value destroyer
People ROI RatioPRR
PRR = Total People Revenue ÷ Total People Cost
How many rupees of People Revenue each rupee of People Cost generates. The single most quotable number in the entire framework — the people equivalent of marketing's "9x."
Benchmark: 1x breakeven · 2x healthy · 3x+ value creator
