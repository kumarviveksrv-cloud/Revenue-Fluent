HUMANCE — The Human Balance Sheet
Finance has a balance sheet that captures intangible value — goodwill, brand, intellectual property. HR has never had an equivalent. The trust, culture, knowledge, and belonging that make a company work have always been invisible to the books, right up until they're destroyed.
HUMANCE is that missing balance sheet. It is a coined currency — a way to put a defensible rupee value on the four intangible human assets that quietly underwrite every rupee of revenue. Revenue tells you what you earned. HUMANCE tells you what made earning possible.
When a founder-led company loses its succession battle and six senior people walk, the revenue drop comes months later. HUMANCE prices the destruction the day it happens — ₹12.1Cr gone before a single sales number moves.
Why it matters
You cannot protect what you cannot measure. HUMANCE makes the intangible measurable, which means it can finally be defended in a budget conversation, tracked over time, and factored into major people decisions before they detonate.
It is the deepest pillar because it explains why the other metrics move. A drop in People Alpha or a spike in attrition cost almost always traces back to HUMANCE destruction — eroded trust, collapsed culture yield, lost signal, vanished belonging.
For any leader weighing a restructure, an acquisition, or a leadership change, HUMANCE answers the question no spreadsheet could: what is the human value at stake, in rupees?
The 4 asset classes
Each instrument isolates a different way this pillar translates people decisions into revenue impact.
Trust CapitalTC
TC = f(knowledge concentration risk, succession readiness)
The value embedded in trusted relationships and the risk when knowledge sits with too few people. Erodes fast when key individuals leave and no one is ready to step in.
Driven by: IRD (knowledge depth) + RBC (succession readiness)
Culture YieldCY
CY = (ESR pre-event − ESR post-event) × revenue base
The revenue yield culture produces — and what evaporates when an event damages it. A drop in employee-sales efficiency after a culture shock is Culture Yield being destroyed in real time.
Driven by: the ESR gap before and after a people event
Signal IntelligenceSI
SI = f(revenue at risk, exit cascade severity)
The value of early-warning signal embedded in your people — the ability to see problems coming. When exits cascade, you lose not just people but the intelligence network that flagged risk early.
Driven by: revenue at risk × severity of the exit cascade
Belonging PremiumBP
BP = exits × discretionary-effort coefficient × revenue base
The premium that genuine belonging adds through discretionary effort — the work people do because they want to, not because they must. It disappears quietly as belonging erodes, taking discretionary revenue with it.
Driven by: exit volume × discretionary-effort impact
