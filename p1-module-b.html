The Gap

Performance marketing can tell you that a specific Facebook ad generated ₹4.2Cr in revenue with 94% confidence. It took 20 years and billions in software investment to build that attribution model.

HR's attribution problem is harder — people decisions have longer time lags, more confounding variables, and no existing measurement infrastructure. But harder doesn't mean impossible. It means the methodology needs to be more rigorous.

The 4-Step Attribution Methodology

This is the intellectual framework that makes People-Revenue Connect possible. Four steps, applied in sequence, to produce a defensible revenue attribution with a confidence score.

1
Map the People Decision Log
Document every significant people decision affecting the business unit — hires, exits, promotions, restructures, compensation changes — with exact dates. This is your data foundation. Without it, attribution is impossible.
TechVelocity: 10 people events across 4 quarters mapped → Month 1 hire, Month 4 exit, Month 7 exit, Month 9 restructure...
2
Apply Time Window Analysis
People decisions don't impact revenue immediately. A bad hire might take 3-6 months to affect team output. An exit might take 6-9 months to show in revenue. The time window varies by decision type and role seniority.
Standard windows: Hire (ramp impact: 3-8 months) · Exit (pipeline impact: 1-3 months, ramp gap: 4-8 months) · Restructure (stability impact: 2-5 months)
3
Adjust for Confounding Variables
Revenue changes are never caused by a single factor. Market shifts, product launches, competitive moves, seasonal patterns — all affect revenue independently of people decisions. You must isolate the people signal from the noise.
TechVelocity: Market index flat in Q3 (+1.2%). No product launches. No major competitive events. People decisions are the primary explanatory variable → high confidence signal.
4
Score Confidence + Calculate Impact
Combine the strength of your time-window correlation, the cleanliness of the confounding variable adjustment, and the quality of your data to produce a confidence score (0-100%). Then calculate the revenue impact.
Output: "The Marcus Webb hiring decision contributed to ₹975K of TechVelocity's ₹1.1M revenue miss. Confidence: 74%."
