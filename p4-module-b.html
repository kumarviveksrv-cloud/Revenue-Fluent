How to Calculate HUMANCE

HUMANCE isn't guesswork. It's a structured 4-step methodology that converts invisible HR effort into a defensible financial proxy. Each step builds on the last.

1
Identify the asset class and its evidence
Which HUMANCE asset class are you measuring? What observable evidence exists that it was built or destroyed? Exit interviews, engagement data, manager feedback, tenure patterns, productivity indices.
Mehta & Sons: Trust Capital evidence → 34yr founder tenure, zero senior exits in prior 5 years, 94% engagement score. Asset existed and was strong before succession.
2
Find the revenue proxy event
HUMANCE is always calculated through its impact on a measurable revenue outcome. Identify the specific business event that demonstrates the asset's value or destruction — exits, productivity drops, client losses, delayed decisions.
Mehta & Sons: Trust Capital destruction → 6 senior exits in 18 months → directly attributable to succession trust collapse. Revenue proxy: attrition cost + productivity loss during transition.
3
Apply the asset class formula
Each asset class has its own calculation formula using the ViRorah metrics. Trust Capital uses IRD + RBC. Culture Yield uses ESR + OSI. Signal Intelligence uses prevention cost methodology. Belonging Premium uses discretionary effort multiplier.
Trust Capital formula: (Institutional Resilience Depth × Revenue at Risk) + (Role Backup Coverage gap × Replacement cost). Applied to Mehta & Sons succession data.
4
State the HUMANCE number with confidence range
Never present HUMANCE as a precise figure. Always present it as a defensible range with your methodology stated. "Trust Capital destruction: ₹2.4Cr to ₹3.1Cr (central estimate ₹2.8Cr). Methodology: IRD + RBC calculation on 6-exit event, 18-month window."
This is what makes HUMANCE credible. You're not claiming false precision. You're claiming structured methodology — which a CFO can engage with.
