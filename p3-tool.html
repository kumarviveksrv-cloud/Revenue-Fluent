Team Revenue Architecture
Your org chart is not an HR document. It is a revenue document. The way teams are structured — how many layers, how wide the span of control, how much autonomy sits where the work happens — directly determines how much revenue the same headcount can generate.
Team Revenue Architecture reads your structure and classifies it into one of three configurations, each with a measured revenue consequence. The same 50 people in Configuration A versus Configuration C can differ by 50%+ in revenue output.
Restructure two teams and you haven't just moved boxes on a chart — you've changed the revenue ceiling. Most leaders never measure this. They feel the drag months later and blame the market.
Why it matters
Reorgs are among the most expensive decisions a company makes, yet they're almost never evaluated on revenue impact. Team Revenue Architecture makes the structure-to-revenue link measurable before you commit to a costly reshuffle.
It also catches the slow killers: every additional management layer adds cost and dilutes accountability; every structural change resets team chemistry and drops the stability index. These compound invisibly until output craters.
For founders scaling fast, this is the difference between adding headcount that compounds and adding headcount that creates coordination drag.
The instruments
Each instrument isolates a different way this pillar translates people decisions into revenue impact.
Org Enablement IndexOEI
OEI = (Revenue-generating roles ÷ Total headcount) × 100
The share of your org actually producing revenue versus coordinating, managing, or supporting. A high OEI means most of your payroll is pointed at the market; a low OEI means you're paying for overhead.
Benchmark: healthy 65%+ · below 50% is structurally top-heavy
Payroll Cost of BurnPCoB
PCoB = f(management layers, span of control)
How much of your burn is consumed by structural overhead — layers and narrow spans of control. It rises with every layer added and every manager overseeing too few people.
Benchmark: lower is leaner · climbs with each added layer
Org Stability IndexOSI
OSI = 100 − (structural changes × churn weight)
Measures how much structural churn is destroying Revenue DNA. Every reorg resets relationships, ownership, and momentum. High change frequency means the structure never stabilises enough to perform.
Benchmark: 80+ stable · below 60 signals destructive churn
