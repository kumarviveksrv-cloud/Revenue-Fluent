The core insight
In finance, "alpha" means return above the market benchmark. A fund that beats the index by 3% is generating alpha. Revenue Fluent applies the same logic to people decisions. Every hire, retention intervention, L&D programme, and promotion either generates above-market return (positive alpha) or destroys value (negative alpha). People Alpha measures which one yours is doing — in percentage terms, against the industry benchmark.
Sterling Consulting promoted four senior consultants simultaneously. All four were high performers. The MD was proud of the decision. Billable utilisation crashed from 78% to 60%. Revenue miss: £2.4M. The same promotions 6 months later, with preparation, would have generated positive alpha.

The 18 metrics

Every metric. Precisely defined.

These are not industry-standard HR metrics. Most of them don't exist in any textbook, any software, or any framework that predates Revenue Fluent. Here is each one — what it measures, how to read it, and what the benchmark means.

P1 — Revenue Connect
ATC% — Attrition Cost Percentage
Total replacement cost ÷ Annual revenue × 100
Converts attrition from a headcount rate into a revenue drag rate. Answers: "For every ₹100 of annual revenue, how much are we spending replacing talent?" A percentage of headcount tells a CFO nothing. ATC% tells them everything.
Healthy: <80% Critical: >140% TechVelocity: 141%
P1 — Revenue Connect
TDF — Talent Drag Factor
Revenue capacity impaired by people in transition ÷ Total revenue capacity
Measures what share of your revenue-generating capacity is currently running below full power because people are ramping, backfilling, or covering. High TDF means your org is structurally running below its revenue ceiling — and nobody is measuring it.
Healthy: <0.2 High drag: >0.5
P1 — Revenue Connect
ESR — Employee-Sales Ratio
Revenue-generating employees ÷ Total headcount × 100
Measures what share of your payroll is pointed at the market versus pointing at itself. A declining ESR is a culture signal — before engagement surveys catch it. When ESR drops 8+ points in a single quarter, a culture intervention is required.
Benchmark: 80%+ Warning: <65% Mehta post-succession: 61%
P2 — People Alpha
Hiring Alpha
Your hire's performance vs industry benchmark for same role
Measures how much better (or worse) your hiring process performs than the market average. Combines time-to-productivity premium and quality premium. A +18% Hiring Alpha means your hires ramp faster and perform better than the market standard — generating measurable additional revenue per head.
Positive: above 0% Negative: below 0%
P2 — People Alpha
Retention Alpha
Value of tenure above market average tenure for the role
Longer tenure compounds. A person in their 4th year at a company generates more revenue per unit of cost than someone in their 1st year — through institutional knowledge, client relationships, and reduced onboarding drag. Retention Alpha monetises that compounding effect.
Strong: +15%+ Market: 0% Negative: tenure gaps
P2 — People Alpha
L&D Alpha
Revenue uplift from learning investment ÷ Programme cost
The only L&D metric that matters to a CFO. Not "training hours per employee" — but "for every rupee we spent on development, how many rupees came back in revenue?" A 24× L&D Alpha (like Sterling's AD programme) is not an HR metric. It's a capital allocation decision with exceptional return.
Strong: 10x+ Acceptable: 3-10x Poor: <2x
P2 — People Alpha
Promotion Alpha
Post-promotion performance vs readiness threshold benchmark
The most commonly negative dimension of People Alpha. When someone is promoted before they're ready (below 70% readiness score), they generate negative alpha — performing below the benchmark in their new role, and dragging the team they now lead. Timing is everything.
Positive: readiness 70%+ Negative: readiness <70% Sterling: -8% at 62%
P3 — Team Architecture
OEI — Org Enablement Index
Revenue-generating roles ÷ Total headcount × 100
Measures what share of your total payroll is actually pointed at the market. Below 50% means more than half your payroll is pointed at itself — through coordination, management, and internal operations. OEI is the single most revealing structural metric in the framework.
Config A: 75%+ Config B: 55-75% Config C: <55%
P3 — Team Architecture
PCoB — Payroll Cost of Bureaucracy
Management + coordination payroll ÷ Total payroll × 100
Measures the structural tax on revenue production. Every layer of management and coordination has a cost — not just in salary, but in the decision-making overhead it creates. Rising PCoB is the leading indicator of a company about to miss revenue targets for structural reasons.
Lean: <30% Heavy: >45%
P3 — Team Architecture
OSI — Org Stability Index
Structural consistency score based on reorganisation frequency
Every reorganisation resets team chemistry, ownership clarity, and the informal networks that make Config A work. Low OSI means your org never settles enough to compound. High OSI means structure is stable enough to develop the Revenue DNA that generates the +34% premium.
Stable: 75+ Destructive: <50
P4 — HUMANCE
Trust Capital
Monetised value of relationship trust held in the organisation
The revenue that flows because clients trust specific people — not the company brand. Trust Capital is the most fragile HUMANCE asset: it can take years to build and dissolve in weeks. When a key person leaves without a trust transfer plan, Trust Capital walks out with them.
Measured via IRD + RBC scores
P4 — HUMANCE
Culture Yield
Revenue generated by cultural efficiency above baseline
Culture is not a feeling. It is a revenue multiplier. Culture Yield measures how much additional output your culture generates above the contractual baseline — the speed of decisions, the quality of execution, the willingness to collaborate without being asked. ESR is its proxy signal.
Proxy: ESR score trending
P4 — HUMANCE
Signal Intelligence
Value of the informal early-warning network
In every organisation, certain people know things before the formal systems do. They know which client is at risk, which deal is stalling, which team member is about to leave. Signal Intelligence monetises this informal network — and reveals how much revenue protection it provides. When these people leave, the early-warning system goes with them.
Rises with tenure + seniority
P4 — HUMANCE
Belonging Premium
Revenue generated by discretionary effort above employment contract
The call made after hours. The client problem solved without being asked. The junior colleague mentored because someone cared. Belonging Premium is the revenue generated by people doing more than their job description requires — because they feel genuinely invested in the organisation. This only happens when belonging is real.
Strong: 15%+ above floor Collapsed: performance at contract floor
P4 — HUMANCE
IRD — Institutional Relationship Depth
% of key relationships that are institutionalised vs personally held
Measures how much of your client and partner trust is held by the organisation itself versus specific individuals. Low IRD is a succession risk, an acquisition risk, and an attrition risk. When IRD is low and a key person leaves, the revenue doesn't stay — it leaves with the person.
Safe: 60%+ Critical: <30% Mehta pre-succession: 18%
P4 — HUMANCE
RBC — Relationship Bridge Coverage
% of key relationships with a named successor/second contact
Measures how many of your revenue-critical relationships have a succession plan. RBC of 20% means 80% of your most important client relationships have no coverage if the primary contact leaves. This is the metric that should be reviewed before every succession event, acquisition, and key departure.
Protected: 70%+ Exposed: <40%
P5 — People P&L
Net People ROI
Total People Revenue − Total People Cost
The bottom line of the People P&L. In absolute rupees. Total People Revenue (everything generated across P1-P4) minus Total People Cost (payroll + attrition leakage). This is the number you bring to the board. Not a percentage. Not a score. A rupee amount that says: this is what the people function contributed to this organisation.
Positive: above ₹0 Negative: people destroy value
P5 — People P&L
People ROI Ratio
Total People Revenue ÷ Total People Cost
For every rupee spent on people, how many rupees come back? 1x is breakeven. 2x means for every ₹100 of people cost, ₹200 of revenue is generated. 3x+ is exceptional. Below 1x means the organisation is a net people value destroyer — even if revenue is growing. This ratio is your seat at the CFO's table.
Strong: 2x+ Adequate: 1.5-2x Weak: <1.5x
How it assembles

Five pillars. One People P&L.

The 5 pillars are not independent. Each one feeds a line item in the People P&L. This is the assembly — how the intelligence system becomes the financial statement.

P1 · Revenue Connect
Calculates revenue at risk from attrition — and the revenue protected through retention interventions. Feeds directly into the revenue side of the P&L.
→ Revenue Protected
P2 · People Alpha
Calculates above-market returns from hiring, retention, L&D, and promotion decisions. Positive alpha = revenue above baseline; negative alpha = revenue destroyed.
→ People Alpha
P3 · Architecture
Calculates the structural revenue premium or drag from the org configuration. Config A adds 34%; Config C subtracts 19%. The structure tax or premium goes into the P&L.
→ Architecture Value
P4 · HUMANCE
Calculates the combined value of all 4 human asset classes — Trust Capital, Culture Yield, Signal Intelligence, and Belonging Premium. The human balance sheet total.
→ HUMANCE Base
P5 · People P&L
Assembles all 4 revenue lines. Subtracts People Cost (payroll + attrition leakage). Outputs Net People ROI and the People ROI Ratio — the two numbers that change every boardroom conversation.
→ Net People ROI
