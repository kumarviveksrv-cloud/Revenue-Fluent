People P&L

HR's Income Statement ~9 min read 4 instruments 🇮🇳 India + 🌎 Global stories
Marketing walks into the board with "9x ROI." Sales walks in with "10.5x." What does HR walk in with?
Engagement scores. Attrition rates. Training hours. Numbers that describe what HR did — not what it returned. The People P&L ends this permanently. It gives HR a number that belongs in the same conversation as every other function's return on capital. And it's built from everything the first four pillars proved.
Beat 1 · The world before
HR has never had an income statement.
Every function in a business eventually has to answer the same question: what did you return on what we spent? Finance answers it with the income statement. Marketing answers it with campaign ROI. Sales answers it with revenue per head. Even Product answers it with feature adoption and retention. HR has never had a satisfactory answer. Not because the return doesn't exist — but because the framework to calculate it didn't.
The HR budget is one of the largest line items in most companies. In a 200-person business at ₹28L average fully-loaded cost, the people budget is ₹56Cr. That is not a support function budget. That is the largest capital allocation decision the business makes. Yet it has historically been evaluated on activity metrics — headcount, retention rate, engagement score — rather than return metrics. The People P&L changes this completely.

Beat 2 · The insight
The People P&L is an assembly job — everything you already know.
The People P&L doesn't introduce new concepts. It assembles the four pillars that came before it into one financial statement. People Revenue is the aggregate of what Pillars 1 through 4 calculated — revenue protected, alpha generated, architecture value delivered, HUMANCE preserved. People Cost is payroll plus the attrition leakage Pillar 1 quantified. Net People ROI is the difference. The intelligence was always there. The P&L is the format that makes it boardroom-ready.
The format matters enormously. A CFO reads a P&L every month. When HR data is presented in P&L format — revenue line, cost line, net line, ratio — it is processed in the same cognitive framework as the finance team's output. It triggers the same questions, the same decisions, and the same accountability. The format is half the battle.
🇦🇺Sydney, AustraliaPeople P&L · Story 2
The negative People P&L that saved a company from itself
James was Group People Director at a 400-person professional services firm in Sydney. The firm had been growing at 15% year-on-year for four years. The founders were considering a Series C raise to fund international expansion.
James ran the firm's first People P&L in preparation for the raise. The numbers were uncomfortable. People Revenue: A$38.2M. People Cost: A$41.1M. Net People ROI: -A$2.9M. People ROI Ratio: 0.93x. The company was spending more on its people than its people were generating in People Revenue. The growth was happening despite a negative People ROI, not because of a positive one.
The diagnosis was clear once the P&L was built: Promotion Alpha was -14% (too many premature promotions into senior roles), and attrition leakage in the Sydney office was consuming A$4.8M annually in TDF drag alone. The revenue growth was masking a structural people inefficiency that would compound through the international expansion.
James brought the People P&L to the founders' strategy session. The Series C was paused for six months while two specific People P&L leaks were fixed. The following year's People ROI: +A$6.1M. The raise then happened — and the investors got the number as part of the deck.

Beat 3 · The world after
The People P&L is HR's permanent seat at the strategy table.
The moment an HR leader can walk into a board meeting with a People P&L — People Revenue line, People Cost line, Net People ROI, ROI Ratio — the entire relationship between HR and the business changes. Not temporarily. Permanently. Because the data now exists, it will be expected in every subsequent meeting. HR becomes accountable to a return, not just a process.
That accountability is not a burden — it is the strategic mandate the profession has been seeking for decades. Every CFO, CEO, and board member understands a P&L. Every one of them can compare a 2.3x People ROI to a 9x marketing ROI and ask the right questions. The People P&L doesn't just give HR a number. It gives HR a conversation partner in every room that matters.
The instruments

How the People P&L assembles.

People Revenue
The aggregate revenue generated and protected by people decisions
PR
PR = P1 (Revenue Protected) + P2 (People Alpha) + P3 (Architecture Value) + P4 (HUMANCE)
People Revenue is the total value-creation output of the HR function — assembled from the upstream four pillars. It converts every pillar's calculation into the revenue side of the income statement. This is the line that has never existed in HR reporting before.
Worked example: TechVelocity: P1 ₹8.3Cr + P2 ₹9.1Cr + P3 ₹5.1Cr + P4 ₹15.5Cr = Total People Revenue: ₹38.0Cr — the revenue HR generated and protected this year.
Represents: everything your people function did that can be traced to a revenue outcome
People Cost
The full cost of the workforce including hidden attrition leakage
PC
PC = Total payroll (PCoB) + Attrition leakage (from ATC%)
People Cost is more than payroll. It includes the revenue that leaked through attrition — the TDF drag, the replacement cost, the institutional knowledge loss. Reporting only payroll understates People Cost and flatters the ROI. The fully-loaded People Cost is the only honest denominator.
Worked example: TechVelocity: Payroll ₹14.4Cr + Attrition leakage ₹2.1Cr = Total People Cost: ₹16.5Cr. The payroll-only number (₹14.4Cr) would have inflated the ROI ratio by 15%.
Includes: fully-loaded payroll plus all revenue leakage attributable to people events
Net People ROI
The bottom line — value created minus value consumed
NPR
NPR = Total People Revenue − Total People Cost
Net People ROI is the single most important number in the Revenue Fluent framework. A positive NPR means your people function is a net value creator — the organisation generates more in People Revenue than it spends on people. A negative NPR means the reverse. This number ends the "HR is a cost centre" argument or confirms it, depending on what it shows.
Worked example: TechVelocity: ₹38.0Cr People Revenue − ₹16.5Cr People Cost = +₹21.5Cr Net People ROI. For every rupee spent on people, ₹2.3 came back in People Revenue.
Benchmark: positive = value creator · 1x = breakeven · 2x+ = strong value creation · 3x+ = exceptional
People ROI Ratio
Revenue generated per rupee of people cost
PRR
PRR = Total People Revenue ÷ Total People Cost
The People ROI Ratio is the most quotable number in the entire framework. It enables the comparison: Marketing 9x. Sales 10.5x. People 2.3x. That comparison — in a board meeting, a CFO conversation, or a strategic review — is the conversation that changes HR's standing permanently.
Worked example: The PRR answers the one question every CFO wants to ask but HR has never been able to answer: "For every rupee we spend on people, how many rupees come back?"
Benchmark: below 1x = value destruction · 1–2x = positive but thin · 2–3x = healthy value creation · 3x+ = best-in-class
Practitioner's Playbook

Five moves for Monday morning.

The People P&L is both a calculation and a presentation. Here's how to build it — and how to use it.
1
Run your first People P&L this month
You don't need perfect data. Run a directional People P&L using estimates where precise data doesn't exist. A directional 2.3x is still a 2.3x. The goal of the first People P&L is not precision — it's to start the conversation and identify where the biggest value leaks are.
2
Present it in the P&L format, not the HR format
Two columns. Revenue side. Cost side. Net line. Ratio. Resist the urge to add HR context, caveats, or methodology footnotes. Present it exactly as a finance P&L would be presented. The format is what triggers the right conversation.
3
Find your biggest People P&L leak first
Your first People P&L will show you where value is leaking. Is it Promotion Alpha dragging the P2 line? Attrition leakage bloating the cost side? HUMANCE destruction collapsing the P4 line? The P&L is a diagnostic — use it to identify the highest-value fix, not to celebrate the overall number.
4
Build a quarterly People P&L cadence
One People P&L is a data point. Four is a trend. Eight is a strategic tool. Commit to running it quarterly — even directionally — and presenting it alongside the business P&L. This creates the accountability loop that makes the number improve over time.
5
Use the People ROI Ratio in every investment case
Every future HR investment request should include the People ROI Ratio impact: "This ₹12L retention programme will improve our Retention Alpha by an estimated 8 points, adding ₹1.4Cr to People Revenue and improving our People ROI Ratio from 2.3x to 2.5x." That is a business case. It will be approved faster than any engagement survey initiative.
Key vocabulary
People P&L
HR's income statement. People Revenue minus People Cost equals Net People ROI — assembled from the four upstream pillars into a single boardroom-ready financial document.
People Revenue (PR)
The aggregate revenue generated and protected by people decisions — the sum of Pillars 1 through 4.
People Cost (PC)
The full cost of the workforce including payroll (PCoB) and attrition leakage (ATC%) — the honest denominator of the People P&L.
Net People ROI (NPR)
People Revenue minus People Cost — the bottom line. Positive means value creator; negative means value destroyer.
People ROI Ratio (PRR)
People Revenue divided by People Cost — the most quotable number. Tells you how many rupees come back for every rupee spent on people.
